# Mental Health

## Mental Health & Chronic Pain

Living with Endometriosis and Adenomyosis affects far more than physical health. Chronic pain, delayed diagnosis, and the daily impact on quality of life can take a significant toll on mental well-being.

**You are not alone, and seeking support is a sign of strength.**

## The Mental Health Impact

Research consistently shows that people with Endometriosis experience higher rates of mental health challenges:

<div class="stat-grid">
<div class="stat-highlight"><span class="stat-number">86%</span><span class="stat-label">experience depression</span></div>
<div class="stat-highlight"><span class="stat-number">29-87%</span><span class="stat-label">report anxiety</span></div>
<div class="stat-highlight"><span class="stat-number">7-10 yr</span><span class="stat-label">average diagnostic delay</span></div>
</div>

Chronic pain fundamentally alters mood, sleep, and social functioning. Diagnostic delays compound frustration, self-doubt, and medical gaslighting. Social isolation increases as pain limits participation in daily activities.

## Coping Strategies

### Daily Practices

- **Pacing:** Break activities into manageable chunks with planned rest periods
- **Pain journaling:** Track pain levels, triggers, and patterns to identify what helps
- **Gentle movement:** Yoga, walking, swimming, or stretching as tolerated
- **Sleep hygiene:** Consistent schedule, dark room, avoid screens before bed
- **Social connection:** Maintain relationships even when pain limits activities; virtual connections count

### Mindfulness & Relaxation

- **Body scan meditation:** Develop awareness of physical sensations without judgment
- **Diaphragmatic breathing:** Activates the parasympathetic nervous system to reduce pain perception
- **Progressive muscle relaxation:** Systematic tension-and-release technique for muscle pain
- **Guided imagery:** Visualization exercises for pain management

<details>
<summary>Caregiver Support (6 tips)</summary>

If you are supporting someone with Endometriosis:

- **Educate yourself:** Understanding the condition helps you provide better support
- **Listen without fixing:** Sometimes people need to be heard, not solved
- **Help with practical tasks:** Cooking, cleaning, errands during flare-ups
- **Attend appointments:** Offer to accompany your loved one to medical visits
- **Take care of yourself:** Caregiver burnout is real; seek your own support when needed
- **Believe them:** Chronic pain is invisible; trust what they tell you about their experience

</details>

<details>
<summary>Workplace & Accommodations (5 tips)</summary>

- Request flexible working hours or remote work options during flare-ups
- Discuss a standing desk, ergonomic chair, or heat pad at your workstation
- In many countries, Endometriosis qualifies for workplace accommodations under disability laws
- Document your needs with a letter from your specialist
- Consider joining employer disability or chronic illness networks for peer support

</details>
